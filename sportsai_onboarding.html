UX Rules — the non-negotiables

These are the design decisions that determine whether onboarding converts or loses users. Every React Native component must follow these.

Rule 01

No step takes more than 20 seconds

If the user has to think, the step is too complex. Maximum: 1 decision per screen. If you need 2 decisions, split into 2 screens.

Rule 02

Never block progress with forced steps

Every step except Sport Selection has a "Skip for now" option. Users who feel trapped leave. The avatar and goal steps are recoverable — they can edit later in profile.

Rule 03

The camera step cannot be skipped easily

Skip is present but visually de-emphasized. Users who film in onboarding have 6× higher 7-day retention. The stat should appear below the skip button as gentle nudge.

Rule 04

Analysis starts while user completes steps 3–4

If they filmed in step 4, start uploading and processing in the background while step 5 celebrations play. When they tap "See analysis" — it should already be done.

Rule 05

Step 5 celebration is mandatory

The belt unlock reveal, XP counter, and "3 things to do now" must animate in sequence. This is the emotional peak of onboarding. No animation here = flat, forgettable experience.

Rule 06

Free for 30 days — no credit card

Show this at the bottom of step 5 without making it a sales pitch. Users should feel they're in, not being sold to. The free trial removes payment anxiety at the highest-engagement moment.
