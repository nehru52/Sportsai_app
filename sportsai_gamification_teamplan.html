Research backing

Why gamification isn't optional

This isn't about making the app "fun." It's about solving the retention problem that kills every sports training app.

Engagement boost

+150% engagement

Gamified apps show up to 150% higher engagement compared to non-gamified equivalents. The brain's reward system responds to progress, achievement, and competition the same way it responds to actual sports.

Retention improvement

+22% retention

Brands using gamification see an average 22% improvement in 30-day retention. Without gamification, Android apps retain just 2.1% of users by day 30. With it, you can hit 20–25%.

The research warning

Excessive gamification backfires

2025 research found that over-gamification causes stress and emotional exhaustion. The rule: gamify the progress journey, not every single interaction. Rewards must feel earned — not handed out.

The single most important insight from research: Leaderboards and status were the most popular gamification features across all sports app studies. Streak mechanics create the strongest daily return behavior. Technique unlocks tap into intrinsic motivation (self-development) which is the only motivation that drives long-term retention — external rewards alone wear off.
Core design

The SportsAI game design system

Six interlocking systems. Each one reinforces the others. Together they create a loop athletes can't easily leave.

⚡ System 1 — Core currency

XP (Technique Points)

Earned by uploading videos, improving scores, completing coach reviews, hitting streaks, and unlocking techniques. XP is the universal currency that powers all other systems. Critically: XP must be earned through actual technique improvement, not just uploading. A video that scores 10% better than your last earns bonus XP. Stagnation earns baseline XP only.

+50 XP — upload + analyze
+120 XP — score improved ≥10%
+200 XP — coach review completed
🏅 System 2 — Identity

Belt / Rank System

Instead of generic "Level 1, Level 2" — borrow from martial arts. White → Yellow → Orange → Green → Blue → Purple → Brown → Black belt. Each belt requires specific technique scores, XP thresholds, and coach-verified milestones. Your belt appears on your profile, in the leaderboard, next to your avatar. It's the single most motivating status signal in combat sports culture.

White belt — getting started
Black belt — elite verified
🔥 System 3 — Daily habit

Training Streaks

Upload at least one video per week to keep your streak alive. A 4-week streak unlocks a bonus technique drill. An 8-week streak unlocks a free coach review. A 16-week streak earns a "Dedicated Athlete" badge visible on leaderboards. Weekly not daily — sports training doesn't happen daily, and unrealistic streaks cause users to quit when they miss one day. Weekly is achievable and still habit-forming.

🎯 System 4 — Direction

Weekly Challenges

Every Monday, 3 new challenges appear. Examples: "Upload a single-leg shot and score above 70," "Get your hip height within 5% of elite average," "Complete a coach review this week," "Improve your entry phase score by 15%." Challenges expire Sunday. Completing all 3 earns a Challenge Champion badge for that week. This creates urgency and direction — athletes know exactly what to work on.

🗺️ System 5 — Progression

Technique Unlock Tree

Athletes don't have access to all techniques from day one. They unlock them by demonstrating mastery at lower levels first. You cannot unlock "Flying Knee" in MMA until your basic striking form scores above 65 consistently. This creates a progression narrative — "I'm working toward unlocking the double-leg" is a more compelling motivation than "I'm improving my wrestling." See full tree in next section.

👑 System 6 — Competition

Leaderboards (3 types)

Global: all athletes by XP — ambition for the top 1%. Regional: athletes in your city/school/club — actually competitive and reachable. Friends: the most motivating — seeing someone you know just ahead of you. Research confirms that regional and social leaderboards drive more behavior change than global ones because the gap feels closeable.

Full mechanic list

Every game mechanic — complete list

What ships in Phase 1 vs what gets added in Phase 2. Don't build everything at once.

Phase 1 — Ship with the MVP (weeks 1–10)

Must ship first

These 5 mechanics are the foundation — nothing else works without them

  • XP system: Points for every action. Shown on home screen, profile, leaderboard. No XP = nothing else is meaningful.
  • Technique score history: Line graph showing your score over sessions. The most motivating thing you can show an athlete is their own improvement. Zero-effort retention.
  • Weekly streak counter: Simple counter on home screen. Breaks if no upload in 7 days. Push notification on day 5: "Your streak ends in 2 days."
  • Belt rank display: Shown on profile and next to avatar. White → Yellow → Orange unlockable in Phase 1. Higher belts gated to Phase 2+.
  • Friends leaderboard: Top 20 friends ranked by weekly XP. The single highest-retention feature you can ship. Build this before the global leaderboard.

Phase 2 — Add after first 200 paying users (months 4–9)

Growth mechanics

Add these once you have data on what's working

  • Weekly challenges: Auto-generated from the athlete's weak phase data. "Your entry phase is your weakness — score above 70 on entry this week."
  • Technique unlock tree: Full sport-specific progression map. Unlocking a new technique sends a push notification to all the athlete's followers.
  • Team/club system: Athletes create or join a team (school team, gym, training group). Team XP aggregates. Team leaderboard. Team challenges. This is the social glue.
  • Achievement badges: "First Perfect Entry," "10-Week Streak," "Elite Hip Height Achieved," "Faster Than Your Coach" (when you outscore your coach's demo). Display on profile.
  • Global leaderboard: Top 100 athletes by technique score per sport. Resets monthly. Top 3 get a "Champion" badge that month.
  • XP multiplier events: "Double XP Weekend" — announced Tuesday, runs Saturday–Sunday. Creates urgency, drives uploads. Zero engineering cost.

Phase 3 — Elite and Olympic tier

Elite engagement

For serious athletes and team contracts

  • Season mode: 12-week competitive seasons with rankings, playoffs, and a "Season Champion" title. Mirrors real competitive sports seasons.
  • Coach endorsements: Coaches can "endorse" athletes, which adds a badge to their profile. Valued by serious athletes for credibility.
  • Technique records: Highest score ever recorded for each technique per sport. Shown on the technique page. "Current record: 97/100 — @elitewrestler." Drives aspiration.
  • Avatar cosmetics: Earn exclusive avatar outfits, belt colors, animations for high-level achievements. Pure vanity, but research confirms it drives retention through ownership.
Technique progression

Technique unlock tree

This is the narrative spine of the game. Athletes are always working toward something. Each sport has its own tree — here's wrestling as the example.

Design rule: Each technique requires a minimum average score on its prerequisites — not just that you attempted them. You cannot unlock the double-leg by uploading one mediocre single-leg. You must average 65+ across 3 sessions. This prevents farming and makes each unlock feel genuinely earned.

Wrestling unlock tree

Stance & Movement 🔓 (starts unlocked)

Basic stance, level change, circle step. Every new user starts here. Score 60+ average to proceed.

Single-Leg Shot

Requires: Stance avg ≥60 across 3 sessions. The core takedown — most wrestlers start here.

Double-Leg Shot

Requires: Single-Leg avg ≥65. More explosive, higher reward, harder to execute correctly.

High-Crotch

Requires: Single-Leg ≥70. The high-percentage NCAA technique — unlocked by serious athletes.

Scramble Defense

Requires: Double-Leg ≥65. Counter-wrestling. Only athletes with solid fundamentals reach here.

Fireman's Carry 🏆

Requires: High-Crotch ≥75 + Black Belt rank. Prestige technique. Fewer than 5% of users unlock this.

MMA unlock tree

Fighting Stance 🔓 (starts unlocked)

Guard position, footwork, head movement. Baseline for all striking analysis.

Jab-Cross Combo

Requires: Stance avg ≥60. Foundation of striking. Most uploaded technique in MMA tier.

Body Hook

Requires: Jab-Cross ≥65. Hip rotation analysis unlocks here.

Takedown Defense

Requires: Stance ≥65. Sprawl mechanics, base defense — grappling layer begins.

Clinch Work

Requires: Takedown Defense ≥65 + Body Hook ≥65. Upper-intermediate MMA.

Flying Knee 🏆

Requires: Clinch ≥75 + 12-week streak. Prestige. A notification goes to all followers when unlocked.

The social unlock mechanic: When an athlete unlocks a prestigious technique (level 5+), their followers get a push notification: "🔓 @username just unlocked Flying Knee — tap to see their latest analysis." This is free viral growth. Every prestigious unlock is a social event.

Leaderboard system design

Three leaderboard types. Each serves a different psychological need. All three must exist — removing any one breaks the system.

TypeScopeResetsRanked byWhy it works
FriendsUsers you followNever (cumulative)Weekly XPThe gap to the person above you feels closeable. Seeing a friend 200 XP ahead triggers competition. Most effective at driving uploads.
RegionalSame city / school / gymMonthlyTechnique score improvement %Ranked by improvement (not absolute score) means beginners can compete with advanced athletes. Reachable, not discouraging.
GlobalAll users, per sportMonthly seasonsCumulative XP + technique milestonesTop 100 visible. Top 3 get a "Champion" badge for that month. Most users won't reach it — but seeing the #1 athlete's score is aspirational.
Anti-cheat rule: XP cannot be earned from repeated identical uploads (same video re-uploaded), scores that don't match pose detection confidence thresholds, or accounts with no coach interaction in 30 days. Gaming the leaderboard destroys it for everyone — build the detection from day one.

Database schema — gamification tables

-- XP and ranking system
CREATE TABLE athlete_xp (
  athlete_id UUID REFERENCES users(id),
  total_xp INTEGER DEFAULT 0,
  weekly_xp INTEGER DEFAULT 0,     -- resets every Monday
  current_belt TEXT DEFAULT 'white',
  belt_progress_pct INTEGER DEFAULT 0,
  current_streak_weeks INTEGER DEFAULT 0,
  longest_streak_weeks INTEGER DEFAULT 0,
  last_upload_date DATE,
  PRIMARY KEY (athlete_id)
);

CREATE TABLE xp_transactions (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
  athlete_id UUID REFERENCES users(id),
  amount INTEGER NOT NULL,
  reason TEXT NOT NULL,            -- 'upload', 'score_improvement', 'coach_review', etc
  source_id UUID,                  -- video_id or review_id that triggered this
  created_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT NOW()
);

CREATE TABLE technique_unlocks (
  athlete_id UUID REFERENCES users(id),
  sport TEXT,
  technique_name TEXT,
  unlocked_at TIMESTAMPTZ,
  unlock_score INTEGER,            -- score that triggered the unlock
  is_prestige BOOLEAN DEFAULT false,
  PRIMARY KEY (athlete_id, sport, technique_name)
);

CREATE TABLE weekly_challenges (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
  week_start DATE,
  sport TEXT,
  challenge_type TEXT,             -- 'score_threshold', 'improvement', 'consistency'
  target_technique TEXT,
  target_value DECIMAL,
  xp_reward INTEGER,
  description TEXT
);

CREATE TABLE challenge_completions (
  athlete_id UUID REFERENCES users(id),
  challenge_id UUID REFERENCES weekly_challenges(id),
  completed_at TIMESTAMPTZ,
  PRIMARY KEY (athlete_id, challenge_id)
);

CREATE TABLE leaderboard_snapshots (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
  snapshot_date DATE,
  leaderboard_type TEXT,           -- 'friends', 'regional', 'global'
  sport TEXT,
  athlete_id UUID REFERENCES users(id),
  rank INTEGER,
  score DECIMAL,
  snapshot_period TEXT             -- 'weekly', 'monthly'
);
3-person team

The team split

Three people, zero overlap, maximum parallel work. Every person owns a complete vertical — they can ship their piece without waiting on anyone else except at defined integration points.

The golden rule of the split: Person 1 builds the engine. Person 2 builds the interface. Person 3 builds the game layer AND collects the data the engine needs. At weeks 6, 10, and 14 they integrate. Between those points, all three work independently.
PersonRoleOwns 100%Delivers toSkill required
Person 1 AI Engineer + Backend Pose service · Rules engine · API · Database · Auth · Payments REST API that P2 and P3 consume Python (ML/CV) · Node.js · Postgres · DevOps
Person 2 Mobile App Developer React Native app · Annotation player · Avatar system · Coach web dashboard The product athletes and coaches actually touch React Native · TypeScript · Three.js · UI/UX
Person 3 Game Designer + Data Engineer Gamification system · Data collection pipeline · Coach recruitment · Labeling XP/challenge API endpoints · Labeled training dataset Game design · Python · Sports domain knowledge · People skills
Person 3 is the most critical hire and the hardest to find. This person needs sports domain expertise (ideally played the sport), can write basic Python for the data pipeline, understands game design enough to spec the XP system, and has the personality to recruit coaches and athletes for the dataset. A pure developer won't do. A pure sports person won't do. Find the hybrid.

Execution

Team sync protocol

Maximum parallelism requires minimum meetings. Three rules.

Daily

Async standup — 3 bullets each

In a shared Slack/Discord channel, every morning: (1) what I shipped yesterday, (2) what I'm building today, (3) any blocker. Read-only. No replies unless it's a blocker. Keeps everyone aware without meetings.

Weekly — 45 min

Monday sync call

Review the dependency map. Confirm each person's deliverable for the week. Resolve any interface disagreements between P1 API and P2 app. That's it. No status updates — those are async. This call is decisions only.

Weeks 6 + 10

Integration sprints — 3 days full sync

All three people working together in person or on a call all day. Week 6: wire P2 annotation player to live P1 AI output. Week 10: full app integration, TestFlight build, beta launch. These two sprints are non-negotiable calendar blocks from day one.

The one thing that kills 3-person teams: unclear API contracts. P1 must publish a Postman collection or OpenAPI spec by end of week 2. P2 builds against mocked responses until the real endpoints are live. If P2 waits for P1 to finish before building screens, you lose 4 weeks. Build against mocks from day one.
