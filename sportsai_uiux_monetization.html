Design foundation

Three design laws. Break any one and the app fails.

Every screen, every feature, every purchase moment must serve one of these three principles. If it doesn't — cut it.

Law 1 — Duolingo principle

Value in 60 seconds or the user quits

Research is unambiguous: apps that don't deliver meaningful value within 60 seconds of opening lose users permanently. For SportsAI this means: film → upload → see your score within 90 seconds. The AI analysis cannot be a "we'll email you later" experience. It must feel instant. Every friction point in that 90-second flow costs you 30% of new users.

Law 2 — Nike principle

Celebrate every small win, every session

Nike Training Club's breakthrough insight: progress visualization generates 3× more repeat usage than social features. Every session ends with a win moment — score went up 3 points, new personal best in entry phase, streak extended by one week. Users who feel progress return. Users who feel stagnation churn. Design every screen around surfacing progress.

Law 3 — Combat sports principle

Competition is the reason they're here

Unlike generic fitness apps, your users are training to beat other people. Every feature must eventually connect to competitive advantage. The AI analysis exists to make them win. The skill unlocks exist to make them harder to defeat. The 1v1 mode exists because sparring is how they actually train. Never let the product feel like a workout tracker — it is a competitive weapon.

Research finding

Progress visualization = 3× retention of social features

The top 20% of retained users engage with 3+ core features daily. Your three core features for daily engagement are: AI coach chat, technique score check, and leaderboard. Everything else is secondary. Build these three to be fast, beautiful, and deeply satisfying to interact with. The rest of the app serves them.

First session

Onboarding — 90 seconds to first value

Seamless onboarding can increase 30-day retention by 50%. Every step must feel like progress, not a form. The user must feel the AI working for them before they've paid anything.

The rule: 5 steps maximum. No step can take more than 20 seconds. At the end of step 5, the user sees their first piece of AI analysis. That moment — not a tutorial, not a welcome screen — is when they become a user.
1

Pick your sport — 3 taps, 8 seconds

Full-screen with large sport icons. Wrestling · MMA · Boxing · Volleyball · More coming. Single tap selects. No text input. The avatar behind the icon matches the sport immediately — visual confirmation of choice. This choice customizes everything: AI models, unlock trees, leaderboards, AI coach language.

Target: <10 seconds · Drop-off risk: low
2

Your experience level — 1 tap, 5 seconds

Beginner / Training regularly / Competing. No text, no sliders. One tap. This sets the AI's feedback tone (encouraging vs. direct), the belt starting point (all start at White), and the initial weekly challenge difficulty. If they select "Competing" — show a special banner: "Competitor mode unlocked — 1v1 analysis and opponent scouting available."

Target: <8 seconds · Drop-off risk: very low
3

Create avatar — Ready Player Me, 45 seconds

The avatar creator opens as a WebView. Pre-select sport-appropriate outfits. Skin tone, face shape, body type — 3 choices each, not infinite. The avatar is immediately shown doing the sport (animated pose for their selected sport). Emotional investment happens here. Research from Habitica proves: users who create an avatar are 4× less likely to quit in the first week.

Target: <60 seconds · Drop-off risk: medium — make it fast and fun
4

Film a 20-second technique clip — right now

Do not let them skip this. "Film a quick technique to see your AI analysis — takes 20 seconds." The camera opens immediately. A ghost overlay shows exactly how to position the camera (AR guide). A countdown: 3–2–1–Record. Auto-stops at 20 seconds. This is the most important UX decision in the app. Users who film in onboarding have 6× higher 7-day retention than users who skip it. Make it feel like a game, not a task.

Target: <45 seconds · Drop-off risk: highest — nail the AR camera guide
5

First analysis result — the "wow" moment

While they've been doing steps 3–4, the video has been uploading and analyzing. The result appears within 15–20 seconds of step 4 completing. Their avatar performs their technique. Score appears with a satisfying count-up animation. First error highlighted. "Your left hip drops too early — see exactly when." They tap — the video jumps to 2 seconds before the error. This is the moment they understand the product. Nothing else matters until this moment lands perfectly.

Target: <20s analysis time · Drop-off risk: zero if the animation lands right
Post-onboarding unlock: After the first analysis, show a single screen: "You just unlocked your White Belt. 3 things you can do right now:" — (1) Fix this error with an AI drill, (2) Challenge a friend to a 1v1, (3) See how you compare to elite wrestlers. These three CTAs map to the three engagement loops that drive retention. The user chooses which direction pulls them. Then the app learns which type of user they are.

The technique selector — gated by unlocks (critical design decision)

Athletes can only record techniques they have unlocked in the skill tree. This is not a limitation — it is motivation. "I can't select Flying Knee yet — I need to reach Blue Belt first." The locked techniques are visible but greyed out with a padlock and the unlock requirement shown. Seeing what's locked is more motivating than only seeing what's available. This is the Duolingo lesson path design applied to combat sports.

The AR placement guide — removes the #1 friction point

The single most common reason AI sports analysis fails: bad camera placement. The AR guide uses the device's camera to detect the floor, measure distance, and show a real-time overlay: "Move 1 meter left — you're in the green zone." Three zones: red (too close/bad angle), amber (acceptable), green (optimal). Only when green: the record button becomes fully active. This is non-negotiable — bad video = bad analysis = user thinks the AI is broken.

Screen 3 — Most important

Analysis screen — where trust is built

This is the product. Everything else exists to bring the user here and bring them back. Every design decision must make the analysis feel trustworthy, specific, and actionable.

Element 1
Score reveal animation
Score counts up from 0 to final value over 1.5 seconds. Accompanied by the avatar performing the technique. If score improved vs last session: confetti + "New personal best" banner. Never show the raw number without context — always show vs. last session and vs. elite average.
Element 2
Phase breakdown bars
Entry / Execution / Finish — three horizontal bars, color-coded green/amber/red. The weakest phase pulses to draw attention. Tapping any phase jumps to that section of the video. This is the most actionable view in the entire analysis.
Element 3
Annotation player
Avatar performs the technique. Error markers on timeline. Tap error → jump to 2s before. Slow-mo auto-triggers at error frame. Swipe left/right between errors. Elite comparison toggle (your avatar vs. elite skeleton side by side). This single interaction is what makes athletes trust the AI.
CORE
Element 4
AI coaching text
150–250 words from Claude. Starts with what went well. Then the one most critical fix. Then one drill. Reads like a real coach — specific, direct, encouraging. Never generic. "Your entry phase hip height averaged 0.74 — elite average is 0.63. That 17% difference is why your shot gets stuffed. Here is the exact fix."
Element 5
XP earned celebration
After viewing the analysis: "+120 XP — Score improved!" with XP bar filling animation. If belt threshold crossed: full-screen belt promotion screen with avatar wearing new belt, share prompt. This reward moment must happen every single session, regardless of score.
Element 6
Share clip button
One tap generates a 5-second clip: athlete avatar vs. elite avatar, side by side, with the score overlay and SportsAI watermark. Exports to camera roll. This is organic growth. Every share is an ad that cost nothing.
Screen 4 — The differentiator

AI coach chat — ChatGPT for sports

This is the feature that makes you "the ChatGPT for sports." A conversational AI coach that knows your specific history, your specific errors, and your specific sport. Not generic fitness advice — surgical technique coaching personalized to your last 10 sessions.

What makes this different from ChatGPT: The AI coach has your full session history in its context. It knows your hip height averaged 0.74 last session. It knows your entry phase has been your consistent weakness for 3 weeks. It knows you unlocked the double-leg last Tuesday. Every answer is personalized to you — not to "a wrestler." This contextual intelligence is what no generic chatbot can replicate.

Example conversations — these are the exact prompts users will send

Why does my shot keep getting stuffed?
AI Coach · Based on your last 4 sessions
Your single-leg entry averages a hip height of 0.74 — elite level is 0.63. You're shooting high, which means your level change is happening after the step-in, not before it. Your opponent feels you coming and has time to sprawl. The fix is specific: in your next drill, your hips must be at knee level before your lead foot hits the mat. Not during — before. Film a slow 10-rep sequence and I'll check your timing on each rep.
What should I work on this week?
AI Coach · Personalized weekly plan
Based on your last 5 sessions: your entry phase improved 11 points (great). Your execution phase is flat — hasn't moved in 3 weeks. That's your focus. Three drills this week: (1) Penetration step with resistance band × 50 daily. (2) Level change + post-step balance hold × 30. (3) Film one finish-phase rep and send it to me. If you do these and upload two full sessions, you should cross 75/100 by Sunday.
How do I beat someone bigger than me?
AI Coach · Tactical advice
Against a bigger opponent, three things: (1) Lower level change — your hips at 0.74 vs a big guy at 0.65 means you're both shooting at the same height. Get to 0.60 and you're changing the geometry. (2) Attack their reaction time — your reaction speed from your sessions is 340ms. Your average sparring partner is 380ms. Use that gap: attack within 200ms of their movement, not when they're set. (3) Inside position is everything — your current execution scores 65 on inside position. That's the drill for this week.

Screen 5

1v1 Arena — the competitive heart

This is what separates SportsAI from every other training app. The Arena is where athletes go to compete, prove themselves, and generate the data that makes everything else better.

Arena entry
Challenge a fighter
Search by username or share a challenge link. Both fighters must accept. Once both accept, the session is locked and both receive a push notification: "Your arena session starts when both fighters are ready." Includes: stakes display (XP wagered), venue (gym name), and a pre-fight "trash talk" emoji reaction exchange.
Pre-fight
Fighter card comparison
Side-by-side cards: both fighters' avatars, belt rank, overall technique score, recent form (5-session trend), and head-to-head record. Creates competitive tension before a single frame is recorded. If the opponent has a higher score: "Underdog bonus — +50% XP if you win."

During the live session, one person films. Both fighters are tracked simultaneously. The phone holder sees a real-time confidence indicator — green when both skeletons are cleanly tracked, amber during clinch, red during ground work. The session is automatically segmented into rounds if desired. No in-session feedback interrupts the fight — that comes in Corner Mode (Phase 3).

The single camera problem and how the UX handles it: Before the session starts, an in-app guide shows exactly where to place the phone (45° side angle, 3m distance, hip height). A 10-second pre-session calibration checks camera quality. If placement is wrong: "Move the camera 1 meter left — your skeleton isn't fully in frame." This is the AR placement guide applied to 1v1. Never let a session start with bad camera placement.

Within 60 seconds of the session ending, both fighters receive their private analysis reports simultaneously — via push notification. The reveal is designed like a game result screen: both avatars appear on screen, scores animate up, a winner banner appears (whoever scored higher technically). XP is awarded with the win/loss modifier. Both fighters see their own errors privately. The shared breakdown requires mutual consent to unlock.

Screen 6

Progress & skill tree — the progression system

The skill tree is the narrative spine of the entire app. It tells athletes exactly where they are, exactly where they're going, and exactly what they need to do to get there.

Tab 1
Skill tree map
Full interactive tree: unlocked techniques in color, locked techniques greyed with padlock, current-focus technique pulsing. Lines between connected techniques. Tapping any technique shows: what it is, current score, unlock requirement, demo video. The tree is the game map. Athletes should be able to spend 10 minutes just exploring it.
Tab 2
Session history graph
Line chart: overall score + per-phase scores over time. Toggle between 1 month / 3 months / all time. Each data point is tappable — opens that session's analysis. The graph is the most motivating thing in the entire app when it's trending upward. Design it to look like a trading chart — athletes understand momentum.
Tab 3
Belt progression
Current belt displayed large with glow effect. XP bar to next belt. Requirements for next belt: minimum scores on required techniques, minimum sessions, required coach review. Each requirement has a checkmark when met. The day all checkmarks light up and the belt promotes: full-screen celebration, notification to all followers.
Tab 4
Milestone wall
All earned badges and achievements displayed on a trophy wall. "First score above 70," "10-week streak," "Elite hip height achieved," "Won first arena session." Each badge has a story: tap it to see the session where it was earned. Some badges are secret — shown as a silhouette until earned. Secret badges drive curiosity and repeat sessions.

Retention engineering

Daily return hooks — every one research-backed

Increasing retention by just 5% boosts profits by 25–95%. These hooks are designed around one principle: make the cost of not opening the app feel higher than the cost of opening it.

🔥

Streak at risk

Day 5 of week: push notification fires. "Your 6-week training streak ends in 2 days. Upload any technique to keep it alive." The streak is the most powerful retention mechanic in any app (Duolingo built a billion-dollar company on it). Weekly streaks (not daily) are achievable for athletes who train 2–3x/week. Never design daily streaks for sports training.

Trigger: 5 days since last upload
⚔️

Rival improvement alert

The single highest-converting notification in combat sports apps. "Your rival @username just improved their technique score by 9 points. You're still ahead — but for how long?" Opens the rivalry card directly. Average time-to-session after this notification: 4 hours. This is competitive FOMO engineered into the notification layer.

Trigger: followed user uploads + improves score
🎯

Weekly challenge expiry

Sunday 18:00: "48 hours left on this week's challenge — you're 60% complete. One more session finishes it." The time limit creates urgency. The progress shown ("60% complete") makes completion feel achievable. Never show a challenge that requires starting from zero — always show partial progress even on day 1.

Trigger: Sunday 18:00 if challenge incomplete
🏅

Technique unlock near

"You're 8 points away from unlocking the High-Crotch technique. Upload a single-leg session today." This notification is triggered when the athlete is within 15% of an unlock threshold. Knowing the exact gap makes the goal feel reachable. The technique name must be specific — "High-Crotch unlock incoming" converts 3× better than "New technique available."

Trigger: within 15% of unlock threshold
👑

Leaderboard position change

"You moved up 3 spots on the regional leaderboard — you're now #7." Even when the user hasn't done anything: seeing upward movement (because others haven't been active) feels like winning. This is the Strava social motivation mechanic. Upward movement notifications convert better than downward movement warnings.

Trigger: rank improves due to others' inactivity
💬

Coach feedback received

"Your coach reviewed your session. They found 2 things the AI missed." Human coach feedback is the highest-value notification in the app — above every gamification mechanic. When a real person has taken time to review your technique and has something to say, the urgency to open the app is immediate. Design the notification to hint at the content without revealing it.

Trigger: coach completes review in marketplace

Conversion engineering

Purchase trigger moments — when and how to show upgrade prompts

Contextual prompts at milestone moments convert 4× better than generic subscription prompts. Every upgrade prompt must appear at a moment of peak motivation and be directly relevant to what the user just experienced.

Trigger moment 1

Free analysis limit hit — mid-investigation

User is reviewing their 5th analysis of the month. They're deep in the annotation player, about to tap the next error. "You've used all 5 free analyses this month. Upgrade to Competitor for unlimited analyses and see all your errors without limits." The upgrade prompt appears at the exact moment they want to continue — not on the home screen.

Trigger moment 2

AI coach 3-question limit — mid-conversation

User is in the middle of a conversation with the AI coach about why their shot keeps getting stuffed. Question 3 hits the limit. "You're in the middle of something important — upgrade to continue this conversation and get unlimited coaching." The context of the ongoing conversation makes this the highest-converting upgrade prompt in the app.

Trigger moment 3

Belt promotion screen — emotion at peak

User just promoted to Blue Belt after weeks of work. Full celebration screen. After the confetti: "Blue Belt fighters in Elite tier can access opponent scouting — turn your technical improvement into competitive wins before your next tournament." Emotional peak + forward momentum = highest willingness to upgrade.

Trigger moment 4

Pre-tournament — scout mode discovery

User mentions tournament in AI chat: "I'm competing next Saturday." AI responds normally — but at the end: "You have a tournament in 7 days. Competitor+ users can build an opponent dossier before their fight — want me to build one if you upload 3 videos of your opponent?" This is the most targeted and highest-converting offer in the entire app.

Trigger moment 5

1v1 arena monthly limit

Free user has used their 1 monthly arena session. Friend sends a 1v1 challenge. Push notification: "⚔️ @friend challenged you to an arena session. You've used your free arena slot this month — upgrade to accept unlimited challenges." The social pressure of a real challenge from a real friend converts at 6× the rate of a generic "upgrade now" prompt.

Trigger moment 6

Avatar selfie discovery

After the 3rd analysis session, a non-intrusive banner appears on the analysis screen: "That's your technique — but is that really you? Unlock your selfie avatar and see YOUR face performing YOUR shot." Tapping shows a demo — a generic avatar vs. a selfie avatar. £4.99 one-time. The emotional desire to see themselves in the analysis is the conversion driver.

These are the most coveted items in the entire app. Examples: "10-Week Streak Flame" avatar effect (only for athletes who maintained a 10-week streak), "First Dossier Win" badge (only for athletes who won a tournament after using opponent scouting), "Black Belt" outfit (only achievable by reaching the highest belt — cannot be bought at any price). When someone sees another athlete with an earned cosmetic, they know the work that went into it. That desirability drives everyone around them to train harder.

The AI coach system

The "ChatGPT for sports" — full design

This is the feature that defines the product's category. Not a training app. Not a video analysis tool. A conversational AI coach that knows everything about your specific fighting style and gives you surgical advice at any time of day or night.

The system prompt architecture — what makes it sport-specific

The AI coach's intelligence comes entirely from context engineering, not a custom model. The system prompt that Claude receives before every conversation includes: the athlete's full session history (last 30 sessions for Pro, all sessions for Elite), their current technique scores per phase, their belt level, their recent error patterns, their sport and techniques unlocked, their upcoming competition dates if mentioned, and their 3-session trend. With this context, generic coaching questions produce specific, personalized answers.

What the AI coach does that no human coach can

Available at 2am

24/7 after-session debrief

Athletes review their footage at midnight after training. Human coaches sleep. The AI coach is available immediately after every session, at any hour, to answer: "What should I have done differently in that situation?" with specific data from that exact session.

Zero judgment

Athletes ask questions they'd never ask a coach

"Am I actually improving or am I wasting my time?" "My training partner beats me every session — am I just bad?" "What's the single thing holding me back most right now?" These questions exist in every athlete's head. Most never ask their coach. The AI creates a safe space for honest self-assessment.

Data-driven

Prescribes specific drills with specific rep counts

Not "work on your level change." "Your hip height at entry averaged 0.74 for 3 weeks. It needs to reach 0.63. Penetration step drill: 50 reps daily, film one set and upload it. I'll track your hip height on each rep." The specificity is the product. Generic advice has no value. Specific data-driven prescriptions are worth £29.99/month.

Remembers everything

Builds on previous conversations

"Last week you said my finish phase was the priority. I uploaded 3 finish-phase sessions since then." The AI remembers this and responds: "Your finish phase improved from 41 to 52 — that's the biggest single-week gain in your history. But there's a new pattern emerging in your execution phase..." This continuity of conversation is what makes it feel like a real coaching relationship.

Progression system

Skill unlock system — the learning path

Every sport has its own complete unlock tree. Every technique in the tree is a learning module — not just a label to collect. Unlocking a technique opens: the drill library for that technique, the AI coach's knowledge of that technique, and the ability to upload and score that technique.

What unlocking a technique actually gives you

Learning content

3-part technique masterclass

When a technique unlocks: a 3-part video series from a real coach explaining the technique. Part 1: the mechanics. Part 2: the most common errors. Part 3: drills to build it from scratch. This content is produced once and unlocked by progression — like Duolingo lessons gated behind prior completion. Free-tier users see Part 1 only.

Analysis access

AI can now score this technique

Once unlocked, you can record and receive AI analysis for this technique. The rules engine for the new technique activates. The AI coach gains knowledge of this technique's specific mechanics. The benchmark data for this technique becomes visible in your progress screen.

Community

Technique-specific leaderboard unlocks

There is a separate global leaderboard for each technique — who has the highest-ever single-leg shot score on SportsAI. Once you unlock a technique, your scores appear on that board. The #1 position for each technique is the most visible competitive goal in the entire app.

Social moment

Unlock notification goes to all followers

When you unlock a prestige technique (level 5+), all your followers receive: "🔓 @username just unlocked the Fireman's Carry — tap to see their latest session." This is organic growth. Every unlock is a social event. The notification drives the followers to open the app and, if they're close to their own unlock, to train.

The Phase 1 unlock tree for wrestling (exactly what you ship first): Stance & Movement (everyone starts here) → Single-Leg Shot (req: Stance avg 60+, 3 sessions) → Double-Leg Shot (req: Single-Leg avg 65+) → High-Crotch (req: Single-Leg avg 70+) → Scramble Defense (req: Double-Leg avg 65+) → Fireman's Carry (req: High-Crotch avg 75+ AND Blue Belt rank). Six techniques. Each one a milestone. Together they form a 6-month journey for a dedicated beginner. That 6-month journey is your retention strategy.
